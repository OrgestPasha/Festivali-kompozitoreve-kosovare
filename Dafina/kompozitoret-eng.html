KOSOVAR COMPOSERS

Lorenc Antoni, one of the first composers in Kosovo, was born in Skopje, Macedonia, in 1909. In his family, where musical art was felt with passion, he discovered and developed his early talent. In the absence of opportunities for formal musical education, he taught himself music and engaged in studying the works of world composers. In his career as a composer and educator, Antoni faced challenges and difficulties, starting his work as a teacher in Ferizaj and later in Prishtina and Prizren. For many years, he conducted choirs and orchestras, contributing to the promotion of artistic and folk music in Kosovo.

Lorenc Antoni


Mulliqi was born on March 18, 1923 in Guci, Montenegro, and attended secondary school at the Medrese in Skopje. He continued his music studies at the Academy of Music in Belgrade, while pursuing his professional engagement as a composer, professor, and conductor in Prishtina and Prizren. He is the founder of artistic music in Kosovo, which has greatly influenced the cultural identity of the country. Inspiration from folklore is evident in his works, but always treated in the context of European creations. His numerous works, such as choral songs, symphonies, and compositions for orchestra, make him one of the pioneers of music in Kosovo and one of the founders of the national style in Albanian music.

Rexho Mulliqi


Mark Kaçinari, as a founder of artistic music in Kosovo, has had a significant influence on the cultural identity of the country. Following the path of innovation and talent, he has created a series of works inspired by Albanian musical folklore, but also with a suitable sense and treatment in the style of classical and romantic European music. His creativity, rich in folk elements and an original creative spirit, is part of Kosovo's musical heritage and has deeply influenced the development of choral and symphonic music in the region. Serving music with passion and dedication, Mark Kaçinari has left an important legacy for future generations of musicians in Kosovo.

Mark Kaçinari


Fahri Beqiri, a respected figure in Kosovo's culture, has contributed to cultural and artistic societies as an artistic leader and founder of music festivals. Born in 1936 in Mitrovica, he has been a teacher in important music institutions in the country. In addition to his pedagogical and creative work, Beqiri has won numerous awards and recognitions at various music festivals and events. He has been an artistic leader in many of them and one of the founders of song festivals in Kosovo such as "Akordet e Kosovës" and "Kosovarja Këndon". His creativity, rich in folk motives, has earned him many awards and accolades.

Fahri Beqiri


Rauf Dhomi is a distinguished composer of operatic works and a founder of the national style of vocal-instrumental forms in Kosovo. Born in Gjakova in 1945, Dhomi has been noted as an important figure in the country's cultural life. After completing his musical secondary education in Prizren and studying at the Faculty of Music Art in Sarajevo, he engaged as a teacher and later as a professor at the Faculty of Music Art in Prishtina. Dhomi is one of the founders of the Composers' Association of Kosovo and has served as the director of the Kosovo Philharmonic. His creativity encompasses various works, ranging from opera and ballet to folk songs and compositions for choir and orchestra.

Rauf Dhomi


Rafet Rudi is a pioneer in Albanian artistic music, bringing a new and innovative spirit. Not only as a composer with his diverse creativity, but also as a conductor, he has been responsible for raising the level of quality of the Kosovo Philharmonic Choir, enriching their repertoire and striving to introduce the Kosovar audience to a new musical style. Rafet Rudi, born in Mitrovica, completed his music studies in Belgrade and later in Sarajevo and Paris, which helped him develop his skills as a composer and conductor. His professional activity has included teaching at the Faculty of Arts in Prishtina and conducting the Choir of Radio Television of Prishtina. Additionally, he is the founder and director of the ReMusica Festival, an important event in the international music scene.

Rafet Rudi


Mendi Mengjiqi, born in 1958 in Upper Lupç, Podujevo, is an outstanding Kosovar composer with a brilliant career in the classical music scene. After completing his studies at the University of Prishtina, he continued his academic studies in Krakow, Poland, where he graduated in composition under the mentorship of Professor Zbigniew Bujarski and obtained his master's degree under the guidance of Prof. Krzysztof Penderecki. Mengjiqi returned to Kosovo to serve as a professor at the Faculty of Arts at the University of Prishtina, where he has taught subjects such as composition, harmony, orchestration, and compositional technique. His career has been honored with numerous awards and accolades from international festivals and competitions. Mengjiqi's music represents an important expression of cultural identity.

Mendi Mengjiqi


Valton Beqiri, born on March 15, 1967 in Mitrovica, is a renowned Kosovar composer. After completing elementary school in Prishtina, he continued his music studies at the University of Prishtina "Hasan Prishtina," graduating in piano and composition. Beqiri is a professor at the University of Prishtina, where he teaches subjects such as polyphony, solfeggio, and applied music. His works have been performed in many countries and by well-known ensembles such as Trio Klavis in Vienna, Klima Quartet in Zagreb, and the Kosovo Sinfonietta. He has also played a significant role in cultural institutions as the Minister of Culture of the Republic of Kosovo and the artistic director of the Kosovo Philharmonic Opera.

Valton Beqiri


Kreshnik Aliçkaj is a well-known composer and professor, born in Peja, Kosovo, who completed his education in Prishtina. After finishing his studies in composition and obtaining his master's degree at the University of Prishtina under the guidance of Professor Mendi Mengjiqi, he now works as a professor at the Faculty of Arts at the University of Prishtina "Hasan Prishtina". Aliçkaj has a rich portfolio of works, including four symphonies, six concertos, a symphonietta, and a cantata titled "Mother Teresa," as well as several chamber works. He has won numerous awards nationally and some internationally. His works have been performed in many countries, including the Vatican, Izmir, Germany, Slovenia, Croatia, Thailand, and others. Some of the most well-known interpretations of his works have been performed by the Kosovo Philharmonic, the RTSH Orchestra in Tirana, and symphony orchestras in Thailand.

Kreshnik Aliqkaj


Drinor Zymberi, born in Peja on July 17, 1987, is an important figure in the Kosovar music scene. After completing high school in Prishtina, he continued his studies at the University of Prishtina "Hasan Prishtina," where he focused more on composition under the guidance of Prof. Mendi Mengjiqi. His works have been presented in many international countries, being appreciated with various awards. As a professor at the University of Prishtina, Zymberi is involved in the youth of music and has created a rich repertoire for orchestras and various ensembles. His works, such as "Symphony," "Trance," and "Refracted Lights," have been interpreted by prestigious ensembles and orchestras worldwide.

Drinor Zymberi
